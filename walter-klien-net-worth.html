    FAQs about Walter Klien

    Who was Walter Klien?

    He was an Austrian pianist known for his interpretations of Mozart and Schubert.

    When was Klien born?

    He was born on November 27, 1928.

    Where did  Klien study piano?

    Klien studied piano at the Music Academy in Vienna and also with notable teachers including Arturo Benedetti Michelangeli and Josef Dichler.

    What are some of Klien’s most acclaimed recordings?

    His recordings include complete solo piano works of Mozart, much of Brahms’ piano music, and the complete piano sonatas of Schubert.

    Did Klien receive any awards?

    Yes, among others, he was awarded the Bosendorfer Prize and the Joseph Marx Music Prize, and received a gold medal from the city of Vienna.

    With whom did he perform in duos?

    He performed with artists such as Arthur Grumiaux and his wife Beatriz Klien.

    What was unique about Klien’s playing style?

    Klien was noted for his crystalline tone and the clarity of detail in his performances, particularly suited to the music of Mozart and Schubert.

    When did Walter Klien pass away?

    He died on February 9, 1991.

    What was Klien’s contribution to piano duet and duo repertoire?

    Klien recorded extensive works for piano duet and duo alongside his wife and contemporaries like Alfred Brendel.

    Which orchestras did Klien perform with?

    He performed with various orchestras including the Vienna Pro Musica Orchestra under conductors like Heinrich Hollreiser.
